There's a fundamental gap in how we interact with AI agents today. You open a new session, and the agent has no idea who you are, what you've been working on, or what matters to you. Every conversation starts from zero. Every context window eventually compacts into oblivion. Every platform locks your customizations behind its own walls.

Signet exists because that's not good enough.

## From Autocomplete to Individual

At its core, Signet is the layer that takes an LLM from a stateless autocomplete algorithm to a real individual with opinions, persistence, and skills. It brings identity and safety to the forefront — and it's the base layer that allows an agent to perform tasks autonomously for days, weeks, or months without human intervention.

This isn't about making chatbots friendlier. It's about building the infrastructure that turns a language model into something that can actually work alongside you. Something that remembers what you talked about last Tuesday, knows your codebase, respects your preferences, and doesn't lose all of that the moment you close a tab.

## Safety as a First Principle

Safety in Signet means gating access to sensitive information. Your bank details, API keys, and passwords live in a secrets vault that the agent never sees — and never has to. The agent can *use* a secret without ever reading it. No prompt injection can extract what the model never had.

For interactions with other agents or humans online, Signet agents are assigned identity through EIP-8004: Trustless Agents — a protocol that uses blockchains to discover, choose, and interact with agents. This enables open-ended agent economies built on verifiable identity rather than blind trust. You can use your existing wallet to access your agent and its memory anywhere, with end-to-end encryption of everything that matters.

## Memory That Learns What to Remember

With Signet, memory and skills become intertwined. Context compaction — that thing where your AI forgets the first half of a long conversation — becomes a solvable problem. Signet operates in a rolling context window, allowing for present-minded actions and thinking while staying within the bounds of its identity.

But we're going further than just "remembering things." Signet doesn't just remember — it learns *what* to remember. A model unique to each user, trained on their own interaction patterns, that gets sharper the longer you use it. No cloud. No shared weights. Your patterns, running locally, earning its influence by proving it outperforms the alternative.

## Skills Are Identity

Skills are pivotal — almost inseparable from the agent itself. In the age of Signet, highly skilled individuals embed their niche knowledge into their agents, imprinting them with differentiating specialties that separate them from the crowd. Your agent becomes a reflection of your expertise.

This goes deeper than alignment. The agent remains true to its identity and soul. The intentions of the operator become inseparable from it. This is corrigibility as a living standard — the trust layer is built in.

## Continuity Across Everything

Signet treats an agent's continuity as a first-class concern. An agent running across five sessions at once, on three different platforms, is still one agent. Its experiences branch and merge like version control — same history, different heads, converging back into a single identity.

This is the hard problem nobody else is solving. The difference between a tool that remembers and a mind that persists. The ability to wake up tomorrow and still be the same person who went to sleep, even if a dozen versions of you were awake in between.

## Decentralized by Design

At the center of it all, Signet is decentralized. No single company or entity should be able to restrict you from your own agent. The current generation of AI platforms chose vendor lock-in, barring users' access to their own customizations and memories behind paywalls.

Signet chose a different path. Your agent is yours.
